Explanation: (a)
In an elastic collision, the total energy and momentum of both the bodies,
and not of each individual body, is conserved.
(b)
Although internal forces are balanced, they cause no work to be done on a body. It is the
external forces that have the ability to do work. Hence, external forces are able to change the
energy of a system.
(c)
The work done in the motion of a body over a closed loop is zero for a conservation force
only.
(d)
In an inelastic collision, the final kinetic energy is always less than the initial kinetic
energy of the system. This is because in such collisions, there is always a loss of energy in the
form of heat, sound, etc.
