9.Why does a cyclist lean to one side, while going along curve? In what direction does
he lean?
Ans:
A cyclist leans while going along curve because a component of normal reaction of the
ground provides him the centripetal force he requires for turning.
He has to lean inwards from his vertical position i.e. towards the centre of the circular path.
10.How does banking of roads reduce wear and tear of the tyres?
Ans:
When a curved road is unbanked force of friction between the tyres and the road
provides the necessary centripetal force. Friction has to be increased which will cause wear
and tear. But when the curved road is banked, a component of normal reaction of the ground
provides the necessary centripetal force which reduces the wear and tear of the tyres.
