Acceleration is given by the slope of the speed-time graph. In the given case, it is given by the
slope of the speed-time graph within the given interval of time.
Since the slope of the given speed-time graph is maximum in interval 2, average acceleration
will be the greatest in this interval.
Height of the curve from the time-axis gives the average speed of the particle. It is clear that
the height is the greatest in interval 3. Hence, average speed of the particle is the greatest in
interval 3.
In interval 1
:
The slope of the speed-time graph is positive. Hence, acceleration is positive. Similarly, the
speed of the particle is positive in this interval.
In interval 2
:
The slope of the speed-time graph is negative. Hence, acceleration is negative in this interval.
However, speed is positive because it is a scalar quantity.
In interval 3
:
The slope of the speed-time graph is zero. Hence, acceleration is zero in this interval.
However, here the particle acquires some uniform speed. It is positive in this interval.
Points A, B, C, and D are all parallel to the time-axis. Hence, the slope is zero at these points.
Therefore, at points A, B, C, and D, acceleration of the particle is zero.
